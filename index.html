Is it time?

Four signs your pipes are telling on themselves.

Most of the homes we repipe were built before the 1970s with galvanized steel. After 50+ years, it fails from the inside out.

SIGN 01

Pinhole leaks keep appearing

One patched leak becomes three. Once corrosion eats through in one spot, the rest of the line is usually right behind it.

SIGN 02

Rusty or brown water

Discolored water first thing in the morning means the inside of your pipes is flaking into what you drink and bathe in.

SIGN 03

Weak water pressure

Galvanized pipe closes up like a clogged artery. If two fixtures can't run at once, the pipe diameter is the culprit.

SIGN 04

A pre-1970s house

If your home still has its original plumbing, a repipe isn't a question of if — it's when. Better planned than flooded.

Common questions

What homeowners ask before pulling the trigger.

How long does a whole-house repipe take?
Most single-story homes are done in one day — crew in around 8:30 AM, water back on by late afternoon. Two-story or larger homes may take a day and a half. You'll know the timeline at the estimate.
Do I need to leave the house during the repipe?
You don't have to, but many customers prefer to be out for the day since the water will be off for most of it. We protect all furniture and floors, so your belongings are safe either way.
Why copper instead of PEX?
Copper lasts 50–70+ years, doesn't degrade in sunlight, won't melt in a fire, adds resale value, and has zero taste or odor concerns. PEX is cheaper to install, but our customers want the permanent solution — not the faster one. We go into detail in the comparison section above.
What does Type L mean?
Copper pipe comes in Types K, L, and M — the difference is wall thickness. Type L is the sweet spot: thick enough for decades of residential use, code-approved everywhere, and what professional plumbers specify. Type M is thinner and cheaper; we don't use it. All our copper is USA-manufactured.
Will there be holes in my walls?
Yes, we cut small access holes to route the new pipes — but we patch every one before we leave. Our reviews consistently say "you couldn't even tell they were there." We don't leave you with drywall dust and a list of things to fix yourself.
How much does a repipe cost?
It depends on the number of fixtures, stories, and the home's layout — which is why we do a free in-person walkthrough before quoting. What we can promise: the price we quote is the price you pay.
